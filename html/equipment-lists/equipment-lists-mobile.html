Overview

Our group has compiled this list of camping gear to make it easier for your child's first night outdoors. Try visiting Atmosphere and MEC for your camping gear.

Camping Backpack

  • Use an camping backpack to hold all of your camping gear.
  • Additional carry-on backpacks are good for hikes and sorting extra gear.
  • DO NOT bring a suitcase!
  • A bag with a size of around 50L should suffice.
  • Here are some examples of camping backpacks you could consider getting.
    • Deuter Fox 40
    • Gregory Stout 45
    • North Face Youth Terra 55L

Night Gear

  • Sleeping bag
    • Temperature rating of -20˚C for winter
    • Temperature rating of -5˚C otherwise
    • Many brands do not use a standardized system to rate sleeping bags
      • This makes the temperature rating inconsistent.
    • Some brands use the “ISO” or “EN” standard to give temperature ratings to sleeping bags.
      • This provides a reliable way to compare sleeping bags.
      • These sleeping bags usually provide a comprehensive list of specifications for customers to make an imformed purchasing decision.
    • Here are some examples of all-season sleeping bags that would be suitable for most of our camps.
      • MEC Draco Jr.-5 Down Sleeping Bag (small bag, check bag size first)
      • North Face Eco Trail Bed -7C
      • Marmot Trestles Sleeping Bag -9°C
      • REI Magma 15
  • Sleeping pad
    • Air pads are more lighter, compact and comfortable, but more expensive. Moisture from breath can get trapped inside, which may eventually lead to degraded performance or bacterial or mold issues.
    • Foam pads are less expensive than air pads, but are heavier, less compact and less comfortable.
    • The R-Value of a sleeping pad measures its capacity to resist heat flow through it. The higher a pad’s R-value, the better it will insulate you from cold surfaces.
      • Ranges from less than 2 (minimally insulated) to 5.5 or more (very well insulated).
      • R-values behave linearly: A pad with an R-value of 2.0 is twice as warm as pad with an R-value of 1.0.
    • For more information, check out REI's article about sleeping pads.
  • Headlight and/or Flashlight
  • Extra batteries
    • Or a power bank with a USB cable depending on if your electronics are chargeable
  • Camping pillow (optional)

Kitchen and Tools

  • Pocket knife
  • Eating utensils
    • With a mesh bag for storing
  • Plates/bowls
  • Mug or cup
  • Water in a water bottle
    • At least 1L
  • Trash/recycling bags

General Clothing

  • Moisture-wicking t-shirts
    • Dri fit shirts or shirts made with material that dries quickly such as polyester.
  • Quick-drying hiking pants
  • Shorts
  • Long-sleeve shirts
    • For reducing exposure to bugs and sun.
  • Lightweight fleece or jacket
  • Hiking shoes or hiking boots
  • Socks
  • Sleepwear
  • Swimsuit (optional/dependant on the camp)
  • Sandals (optional)

Rain and Cold Gear

  • Rain-jacket
    • Ideally a water-proof Gortex jacket
  • Water-resistent pants
  • Base-layer pants
  • Insulated jacket or vest
  • Gloves
  • Warm hat

Health and Hygiene

  • Toothbrush and toothpaste
  • First aid kit
  • Hand-sanitizer (optional)
  • Toilet paper (optional)
  • Towel (optional)
  • Sunglasses (optional)
  • Sunscreen (optional)
  • Insect repellent (optional)
